About

Batangas Province, located in the Calabarzon region of the Philippines, is one of the country’s most popular travel destinations known for its blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. Just a few hours away from Metro Manila, Batangas offers travelers pristine beaches, scenic mountains, serene lakes, and vibrant dive spots. From the white sands of Laiya and the clear waters of Anilao to the panoramic views atop Mt. Maculot, the province continues to attract both local and international visitors seeking adventure, relaxation, and spiritual experiences.

Beyond its natural wonders, Batangas is also rich in heritage and faith, reflected in its many centuries-old churches that stand as symbols of devotion and resilience. Among the most visited are the Minor Basilica of Saint Martin of Tours (Taal Basilica)—the largest Catholic church in Asia, the National Shrine of Saint Padre Pio in Sto. Tomas—famous for its healing masses and peaceful atmosphere, and the Our Lady of Caysasay Shrine, one of the oldest Marian pilgrimage sites in the country. These sacred landmarks not only serve as places of worship but also as cultural treasures that showcase Batangas’ deep-rooted spirituality and history.

With its combination of stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and strong religious devotion, Batangas has rightfully earned its place as a top destination in Southern Luzon. Whether you’re looking to explore its beaches and mountains, experience its vibrant festivals, or visit its remarkable churches, Batangas offers a journey filled with beauty, faith, and discovery.
