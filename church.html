Minor Basilica of Saint Martin of Tours (Taal)

The Minor Basilica of Saint Martin of Tours, also known as the Taal Basilica, is the largest Catholic church in the Philippines and in Asia. This historic Baroque landmark overlooks Taal Lake and stands as a symbol of faith and heritage for Batangueños.

National Shrine of Saint Padre Pio (Sto. Tomas)

The National Shrine of Saint Padre Pio in Sto. Tomas, Batangas, is a major pilgrimage site known for its open-air design and peaceful atmosphere, where devotees seek healing and blessings.

Our Lady of Caysasay Shrine (Taal)

The Our Lady of Caysasay Shrine in Taal is dedicated to the miraculous image of the Blessed Virgin Mary, believed to have appeared in the 1600s. It remains one of the most revered Marian pilgrimage sites in the Philippines.

Basilica of the Immaculate Conception (Batangas City)

One of the oldest churches in the province, the Basilica of the Immaculate Conception serves as the seat of the Archdiocese of Lipa and stands as a center of devotion and heritage.

San Sebastian Cathedral (Lipa City)

This grand cathedral in Lipa City serves as the seat of the Diocese of Lipa and is dedicated to Saint Sebastian. Its architecture and solemn ambiance make it a beloved site of worship.

Montemaria Shrine (Batangas City)

The Montemaria Shrine is home to the towering Mother of All Asia statue overlooking Batangas Bay, offering a breathtaking view and a peaceful place for prayer and reflection.
