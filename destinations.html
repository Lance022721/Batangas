Batangas Province

Batangas Province, located in the Calabarzon region of the Philippines, is known for its rich culture, beautiful beaches, and strong local traditions. It’s famous for scenic spots like Taal Volcano—the world’s smallest active volcano—and for its heritage towns like Taal, its kapeng barako, and the warm, hardworking Batangueño people.

Laiya Beach (San Juan)

Laiya Beach in San Juan, Batangas is one of the province’s most popular beach destinations, known for its long stretch of white sand, clear blue waters, and relaxing tropical vibe. It’s a favorite weekend getaway for locals and tourists alike.

Mt. Maculot (Cuenca)

Mt. Maculot in Cuenca, Batangas is a well-known hiking destination, especially popular among beginner and seasoned hikers alike. It’s famous for the “Rockies,” a cliffside viewpoint that offers a breathtaking panoramic view of Taal Lake and Volcano.

Verde Island (Batangas City)

Verde Island in Batangas City is known as the “Center of Marine Biodiversity” in the world. It’s famous for its crystal-clear waters, colorful coral reefs, and abundant marine life, making it a top destination for diving and snorkeling.

Taal Volcano (Talisay)

Taal Volcano in Talisay, Batangas is one of the smallest active volcanoes in the world, known for its stunning view and unique location within Taal Lake.
